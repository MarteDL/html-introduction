A funny story

A couple of years ago I moved to England to work there as an au pair. It took me about 6 months and a very awkward moment in the fruit ile to discover that pomegranate was not pronounced as a french apple (pomme) followed by a throwing weapon (grenade). I found out I had been mispronouncing it in the middle of a supermarket when I said it wrong in front of the eldest child of the family I stayed with. She was so kind to loudly laugh in my face for 5 minutes. Safe to say I now always say [POM] + [I] + [GRAN] + [IT] instead.

TL;DR

When I lived in England I mispronounced pomegranate for 6 months before a 14-year old laughed in my face and taught me how to say it correctly.
